Borders of Defiance

With a profound interest in geopolitics and the intricate balance between nations and borders, I embarked on a creative journey—an exploration through the digital realm that led me to urban landscapes across the world. During this expedition, I stumbled upon the island of Hispaniola, a place of profound historical significance. This island, shared by two nations, Haiti, and the Dominican Republic, piqued my curiosity. Despite inhabiting the same landmass, these neighbouring countries have developed vastly differing cultures, languages, and histories. "Borders of Defiance" is the culmination of my exploration into their shared and divided past.

The island of Hispaniola itself became a potent symbol that guided my design choices—a visual representation of the division between these two nations, each possessing its own distinct culture, history, and ongoing struggles. Throughout the design process, I conducted experiments with various elements, meticulously considering the role of each detail in authentically conveying the narrative. Extensive printing tests helped me determine the optimal approach to preserve the story's essence.

A striking feature of the book is its cover, painstakingly laser-engraved with the shape of Hispaniola, prominently displaying the border that separates the two nations. The title, "Borders of Defiance," serves as a commanding introduction to the book's central theme. The choice of materials was a pivotal decision. I opted for a robust carton-based material for the cover, enabling precise engraving and adding depth to the symbolism of borders through visible lines.

The project posed unique challenges, including decisions about the book's title and the selection of words and captions. These challenges served as opportunities to reinforce the message I aimed to convey, prioritizing empowerment of those who endured struggles and displacement over glorifying the colonizers.

Having exhibited this book at a school event, where it garnered significant interest, my future plans include delving into more untold stories of diverse communities worldwide. I aspire to create projects that enlighten people about the histories and struggles of these communities, fostering awareness of how we can contribute to their well-being.

This project has been a profound learning experience for me as a designer. Delving deeply into the intricate histories of Haiti and the Dominican Republic, I gained a profound understanding of the enduring impacts of colonialism and displacement. Through the creative process, I've come to appreciate the immense power of design in conveying meaningful messages and stories. My hope is that this project serves not only as an informative resource but also as a source of inspiration, igniting conversations about unity, division, and the remarkable resilience of communities in the face of adversity.
