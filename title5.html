Emotion Website

With this project I aimed to bridge the gap between student expats and a vast historic collection of videos and pictures within a digital archive. To achieve this, my partner and I devised a unique approachâ€”we sought to connect users with this archive through their emotions. This approach was particularly fitting for student expats, who often find themselves in a new country, navigating a sea of unfamiliar emotions.

Our inspiration led us to develop a website interface, driven by my interest in coding. We envisioned a digital platform where expats could express their emotions, leading them on a journey through the archive's rich content. Sound played a pivotal role in enhancing the interactive experience, strengthening the emotional connection for users.

Our journey began with comprehensive research on our target user group, delving into their characteristics and shared emotional experiences. We then moved on to prototype and test our ideas with the student expats, refining our website's design and usability iteratively. Continuous improvement was the key to crafting a more user-friendly and engaging platform.

The standout elements of our project were the six emotion-driven pathways: Anger, Sadness, Happiness, Disgust, Surprise, and Fear. Using machine learning, we enabled the website to recognize and respond to objects representing these six emotions shown to the camera. Each emotion led users to a corresponding page within the archive, creating a thematic experience enhanced by a carefully crafted soundscape sourced from the archive itself. Our primary tool was code, which we used to build the website interface and enable its interactive features.

Working collaboratively presented its own set of challenges, particularly in aligning our visions and distributing the workload. Through extensive discussions and collaboration, we successfully navigated these challenges, ultimately creating an engaging, interactive website to help student expats connect with the archive.

Our project aimed to foster a deeper connection between student expats and a historic archive by using emotions as a bridge. Through our website, we sought to empower users to explore the archive's rich content and find resonance with their own emotional experiences. The feedback we received was overwhelmingly positive, with users commending the website's ability to provide a unique and emotionally resonant entry point into the historic archive.

In the end, our project proved successful in creating an interactive website through machine learning, enabling student expats to connect with the archive through the lens of their own emotions. This achievement underscored the potential of emotions as a powerful conduit for engaging with history and forging meaningful connections with the past. This project stands as a testament to our commitment to innovation and our belief in the power of emotions to shape our understanding of the world around us.
