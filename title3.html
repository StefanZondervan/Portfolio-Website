Stefan Zondervan

Percipi

In the realm of artistic collaboration, a group of creative minds, including myself, embarked on a project that would challenge conventional thinking and question the pervasive influence of the spectacle in modern society. This project sought to create an interactive installation that delved into the profound ways in which the spectacle shapes an individual's self-identity—a concept deeply inspired by the theories of Guy Debord. With a critical and informative tone, we endeavoured to provoke introspection and awareness: how does the spectacle influence our personal image, behaviour, and identity?

Our group embarked on this artistic endeavour with a shared vision—to construct an installation that engaged all the senses of the body, transporting participants beyond the world of the spectacle and into a realm of reflection. The process began with meticulous planning, as we carefully designed the layout and elements of the installation. Every decision was made with a singular purpose: to enhance our message and create a profound experience for those who engaged with it.

Several key design elements converged to bring our vision to life. We crafted a mesmerizing soundscape, a harmonious blend of a recorded heartbeat and our voices expounding upon the theory of the spectacle and the individual's perception of the world. This auditory journey was integrated with a mirror placed with a thin layer of water on it, with a hidden speaker beneath. The resonating music caused ripples in the water, transforming the mirror into a dynamic canvas that reflected the world in a unique and thought-provoking manner. Illuminated by a single light source, the mirror became a captivating portal for introspection.

To realize our vision, we constructed a custom frame to hold the mirror in place and facilitate the subtle vibrations induced by the speaker. The music, an integral part of the experience, was meticulously composed and recorded by our group, with the inclusion of our own heartbeats to enhance the immersive quality of the soundscape. The installation space was carefully selected to maximize the impact of the reflection on the ceiling, creating an ambiance that encouraged deep contemplation.

In tandem with the installation, we crafted a publication that served as a companion piece, diving deeper into the theories of Guy Debord and the concept of the spectacle. In this publication, we engaged in critical theorizing, exploring the multifaceted meanings of the spectacle through a blend of visuals and narratives, all created by our own hands.

The publication took the form of a leporello—an intricately folded book that invites readers to unfold it, revealing a larger canvas that connects with the theory of the spectacle and our installation. The cover of this book held a special significance, as it is a mirror—a mirror that offered a reflection, but not one clear enough to erase the ambiguity. This mirror echoed our core message—how one endeavour perceives themselves in the context of the spectacle and how this influence shapes one's identity.

Working within a group of five individuals presented its own set of challenges. The necessity to align our ideas and roles, while adhering to deadlines, required extensive communication and discussion. However, through dialogue and collective effort, we successfully navigated these challenges, allowing our creative vision to flourish.

At the heart of our installation and publication was a profound message—to encourage individuals to become more conscious of the way the spectacle, in its various forms, shapes our self-identity. Through abstraction, sensory engagement, and critical theorizing, we aimed to provoke contemplation and initiate a critical dialogue about the influence of the spectacle in modern society. As a collaborative effort, this project served as a testament to the power of artistic expression and the potential for art to inspire introspection. Our abstract exploration, "Percipi," sought to peel back the layers of the world we perceive and invite viewers to reflect on the world within.
